## Overview  
This project presents a Marketing Campaign Dashboard developed using Microsoft Power BI.  
It provides interactive insights into campaign performance, customer behavior, and sales channels.

The objective is to enable data-driven marketing decisions, optimize campaign strategies, and improve ROI.

## Context  
Marketing teams often manage multiple campaigns across different platforms but lack a unified view of performance.  
This can lead to inefficient spending and missed opportunities.

This dashboard centralizes campaign and customer data to help businesses:
- Track performance  
- Understand customer segments  
- Improve targeting and conversions  

---

## Business Questions  

- Which campaigns perform the best?  
- What channels drive the most sales?  
- Which product categories generate the highest revenue?  
- How do demographics affect purchasing behavior?  
- What is the relationship between web visits and purchases?  
- Which customer segments should be targeted?  

---

## Insights  

### Campaign Performance  
- Campaign 4 has the highest acceptance rate  
- Campaigns 3 and 5 perform well  
- Campaign 2 is the weakest performer  

### Sales & Channel Performance  
- Store is the top-performing channel  
- Web traffic is high but conversion is lower  
- Catalog and Deals contribute less  

### Product Category Trends  
- Wines generate the most revenue (~679K)  
- Meat is the second-highest category  
- Fruits contribute the least  

### Customer Demographics  
- Total Customers: 2,240  
- Avg Age: 57  
- Avg Income: $52,247  
- Majority are married and college-educated  

### Customer Behavior  
- Customers aged 50+ spend more  
- Mid-to-high income groups are key buyers  
- Web visits do not always convert into purchases  

---

## Recommendations  

- Scale high-performing campaigns (Campaign 4)  
- Optimize or replace low-performing campaigns (Campaign 2)  
- Improve web conversion rates  
- Focus on top product categories (Wines, Meat)  
- Target high-value segments (50+, higher income)  
- Use A/B testing for campaign improvements
